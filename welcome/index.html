welcome

a note to kick it off…

Admittedly, sharing your thoughts to the world with no one to read them feels inherently meaningless—and if I shared anything at all under the assumption that people would care to read, I’ll question: what gives me, someone the world barely knows, the confidence and sense of self-importance to expect a careful audience?

I think the answer is that there really is nothing. What brings you to flip through these words letter by letter is unknown to me. You might be curious, bored, or someone I know who’s making an effort to support my spontaneous efforts (thank you <3). No matter the reason, I’m grateful that you’re here reading this little bubble of thought of mine.

My background in writing is limited to mostly mundane academic pieces, incoherent poetry, and the private journals I’ve kept since my emotions welled up enough to overflow onto a page—so please bear with me… The first deep thought I'd ever recorded was in 2018 (I was twelve). The only real experience I remember from back then was that of having the consciousness to be insecure, which was reflected through the pages of my journal as they went on. Much of my writing from then created a perfect visualization of self-loathing: I was hit with problems larger than playing detective to figure out who kissed who at the school dance. Family feuds, identity crises, unfruitful relationships and the impending doom of high school weighed me down to what had felt like the ocean floor.

Sure, it was not that deep—but it lowkey was… my past dread is just as dreadful in the present.

In retrospect, I was just one of the many few young people experiencing difficulties from almost every angle of life—but if I had the chance to speak to my past self, I wouldn’t. Contrary to what many people would expect, I think it’s nicer to leave things of the past untouched and permanent—especially the important things. They’re the blocks at the foundation of maturity. They pile on top of each other slowly to create me. And as imperfect as I am, I like me. I thank my 12 year-old self for that.

My writings—the ones I choose to share with you—are therefore my small lines of evidence and permanence to the world. What I leave here and what I leave to myself will be incredibly interesting for me to interpret and look back on 5-10 years down the line.

So going back to the first question: what could reading my ramblings be to you? The truth is, at the end of the day, I think we all live in these little bubbles. Floating about with our own hopes, worries, dreams… or lack thereof. In our little bubbles, we feel so profoundly and so often look at the world through our windows. And the world might just end if somebody crawled through without a warning. But I think I’d like to open my door a crack, and I welcome you to poke your head inside if you’re curious. I don’t know what you’ll find if you do, because I can’t tell you where to look. But what I can do is simply let you. Regardless, if you decide to stop by, this is my welcome to you, with an open arm and a cup of London Fog (because what better time is there to dwell than with a cup of tea?) We’ll sip it together, if you’ll listen. And I sincerely hope that between the tea and our patience, you’ll hear me listening too.
